5 Signs Your Toronto Business Needs to Outsource Billing

How to know when your billing process is costing you more than outsourcing would.

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ Tech Chimps Team

For most small and mid-size businesses, billing starts as a founder task. You send invoices from a spreadsheet, follow up with clients manually, and reconcile payments at the end of the month. It works — until it doesn't.

The problem is that billing problems are slow to show up. A few late invoices here, a missed payment there, and suddenly you're sitting on $40,000 in outstanding receivables wondering where the cash went. By the time it's obvious that something needs to change, the damage is already done.

Here are five signs that your billing process has outgrown what you can handle in-house — and what to do about it.

1. Your Invoices Are Consistently Late

If you're regularly sending invoices more than a week after a service is delivered, you're giving clients a reason to delay payment. Late invoicing signals to clients — consciously or not — that cash flow isn't urgent to you. And if it's not urgent to you, it won't be urgent to them.

For service businesses, every day an invoice isn't sent is a day you're extending free credit. If invoices go out 10 days late and your payment terms are net 30, you're actually operating on net 40 — or longer.

A real estate management firm we work with was invoicing 3 weeks behind their service delivery dates. After we took over billing, they closed that gap entirely within the first month. Collections improved 25% in 60 days.

2. You're Spending More Than 5 Hours a Week on Billing Admin

Track your time for one week. Count every minute spent creating invoices, following up on payments, reconciling accounts, or dealing with billing disputes. If that number exceeds five hours, you have a problem.

At five hours a week, you're spending 260 hours a year — more than six full work weeks — on billing administration. For a founder billing at even $100/hr opportunity cost, that's $26,000 in lost productivity annually. You could outsource all of it for a fraction of that.

3. You Have Outstanding Receivables Older Than 60 Days

Every account receivable over 60 days is a warning sign. Over 90 days, and you're statistically likely to never collect the full amount. Most businesses write off 20–30% of receivables that age beyond 90 days.

The solution isn't to get tougher with clients — it's to have a consistent, automated follow-up sequence that goes out without fail. Outsourced billing teams do this as a matter of course. Your part-time bookkeeper or spreadsheet doesn't.

4. You've Made Billing Errors That Cost You Money or Relationships

Billing a client twice. Sending an invoice to the wrong company. Applying a payment to the wrong account. These mistakes happen in manual systems — and they erode client trust in ways that take months to repair.

If you've had even one of these incidents in the past year, your billing system isn't reliable enough for the volume of business you're doing. The cost of the error is almost always higher than the cost of prevention.

5. Your Bookkeeper Is Doing Billing — and Neither Job Is Getting Done Well

Many small businesses have a part-time bookkeeper who "also handles billing." The reality is that bookkeeping and billing management are different disciplines that require different levels of attention. When one person is doing both reactively, both suffer.

Billing is a client-facing function with real revenue consequences. It deserves dedicated attention from someone whose entire focus is making sure money moves on time and disputes get resolved quickly.

What to Do Next

If three or more of these signs apply to your business, it's worth having a conversation about what outsourced billing actually looks like in practice — the cost, the process, and what you'd actually get back in return.
